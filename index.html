What I Do

I help other artists with their visual communication—specifically photography, video, and websites. I can't help you figure out who you are or what you want to say, but once you know that, I provide the technical rigour to ensure you show it properly to the world.

Services

Photography

I provide high-fidelity digitization of physical artworks, capturing the fine textures and color accuracy essential for professional archives and high-end reproduction. With over 1,000 works documented, my process ensures that the transition from physical medium to digital file preserves the integrity of your original vision. Beyond studio documentation, I also provide professional coverage for artworks displayed in exhibition or gallery settings, delivering files ready for catalogs, digital portfolios, or to make your own professional prints.

Video Production

I craft short-format visual stories that bridge the gap between the artist's studio and the public eye through mini-documentaries, demos, and high-impact reels. My background in cinematography allows me to frame your creative process with a narrative depth that standard documentation often misses. These videos are designed to be "hero" content—capturing the movement, sound, and atmosphere of your practice to engage viewers instantly.

Websites

I build specialized artist platforms using a professional-grade stack that eliminates recurring platform fees and puts total ownership back into your hands. Leveraging the same architecture as poetonic.com, these sites are designed to be fast, secure, and effortlessly manageable through a custom-tailored interface. The result is a sophisticated online home where your work, your shop, and your story live permanently without the burden of monthly subscriptions.

About Don Samec

Don Samec is a California-born French mixed-media artist, photographer, and creative producer based in Cork, Ireland. With an MA in Cinematography from E.S.E.C. Paris and over a decade of international experience, he brings technical fluency to artistic practice. Having documented over 1,000 artworks and exhibited globally, he understands the visual rigour required to represent an artist’s work in the contemporary era.
